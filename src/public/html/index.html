Internet Scams

Notificaion Spam/Scams

These notifcations are usually trying to convince you to think they are notifications from the computer. Yet these are usually just notifications from a malicious website.

How To Remove:

Can usually be removed from Browser notification settings or Computer notification settings

    Links to Broswer Notification Settings (Enter these links into the address bar)
  • Google Chrome:

    chrome://settings/content/notifications

  • Mozilla Firefox:

    :preferences#privacy

  • Microsoft Edge:

    edge://settings/content/notifications

    • Fake Login Websites

    These websites attempt to trick you into giving over your personal information. They are usually used by scam websites to steal your personal information. Once the login is done it usually will send you to an error page or just the main page of the actually login website.

    How To Avoid:

    It's best to avoid these websites. To test the login, you can put in fake information and it will act as if you put your actuall information in it because the scam website doesn't know your actuall information.


    Using Multi-Factor Authentication is also a great way to prevent people from logging into your accounts.

    Sim Swapping

    Sim Swapping is a type of scam where a fraudster convinces a phone carrier to transfer your phone number to a SIM card under their control. Once they have control of your phone number, they can intercept your calls, texts, and even access accounts linked to your phone number, potentially leading to identity theft or financial loss.

      How To Avoid:

  • Use Strong Authentication

    Enable multi-Factor Authenication (MFA) and perferably use password manager apps such as KeyPass

  • Be Cautious of Suspicious Requests

    Unexpected calls, emails, or messages requesting personal information or account details could be used for a Sim Swapping attack.

    • Tech Support Scams

    Scammers will create websites that look identical to legitimate ones, such as banking or online shopping sites, to trick users into entering their login credentials or payment information. Tech Support scam websites in particular will have people on a call support service that will have you connect to them with a desktop sharing software. Yet this let's them have access to your computer, so they could steal plenty of information. There are many people that mess with these types of scams, such as Jim Browning.

      How To Avoid:

  • Make sure you are on the correct website by making sure the url is correct and has https:// instead of http://
  • Be skeptical of any pop-ups. It's best to avoid these and use an ad-blocker such as ublock origin.
  • Tech Support will not need payments in gift cards.

    • Overall, these are just a few examples of the many different types of scams that exist on the internet. Stay Safe and Always Be Skeptical.
